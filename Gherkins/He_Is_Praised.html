Welcome, and congratulations on resisting the influence of the devilish gnomes by praising the Gherkin. Welcome, disciple, to the lexicon of the Gherkin. Within you will find access to the collective knowledge on the Gherkin, ally of man, enemy of Gnome. Read on to delve into their secretive history, and learn how to live alongside our minute friends. Soon, perhaps, you may even meet a Gherkin in the flesh.
The Gherkin is a small creature which lives deep within tall grasses and great swaths of nature. They taught man to plow the fields and grow life from seeds. They ride toads and frogs as men ride steeds, and oft depicted in combat with creatures such as snails. This is, in fact, the origin of Byzantine era paintings of warriors battling snails, though the Gherkins were poorly represented as many artists commissioned could not see a Gherkin. They were likely told the Gherkin resembled a 'very small man'.

A common misconception about the Gnome is gained from the common garden gnome. Why, indeed, should we hate a creature that we place around our gardens? Are they not an ally to the Gherkin? They are often believed to be a protective charm.
Whilst the garden gnome is indeed a protective charm to the garden, this is not because the gnome is inherently good. The garden gnome is, in fact, taking advantage of one of the two only emotions a Gnome can feel. These being 'hate' and 'fear'. The garden gnome is a warning. A show of dominance. It exhibits to the gnomes what will certainly happen to them if they tread upon this territory. They will avoid the garden because of this, taking with them their mealy worms and other parasites.
Now many you know may certainly have become influenced by the silvered tongue of the Gnome. They offer much, and deliver on even more. You may view your friends and they appear happy, content. So how could the gnomes be such devils, if they fulfill their promises?
There is a phrase known to many in our circle, the phrase is simple. The Devil never lies. The devil offers fruits of joy. He opens the gates of a veritable Eden of pleasure, and you may indeed freely pick of its fruits. In this way, the gnome is much like the devil. They promised the intelligent science. They promised the fearful and uncertain security and they promised the sick sterility. As you can see, we live in such a world today. Man is rich, richer than he has ever been. However, wealth in material is all the gnome can offer.
Our world is filled with science at the expense of our spirituality. Intellect is chosen before wisdom. Security is offered at the expense of constant surveillance. Sterility is given at the expense of our health. They have offered us, and you, a quick way to immediately gain something without effort. Anything gained without effort is gained without certainty.
The Gherkin has not taught us this directly. They haven't a language complex enough to convey such notions or ideas. The Gherkin has taught us in the best way that any teacher can. Rather than show us the answer before the question, they gave us a situation. They gave us a problem, and gave us the path to the solution. The solution was reached by us, and thus the solution was earned.
